Course Info

CS 426 - Senior Projects

Course Outline

A continuation of CS425 Software Engineering, this capstone course emphasizes team collaboration and application of modern engineering approaches to software construction. The development by each team of an original, industry-strength software-intensive product is the main objective of the course. The teams will report on their project’s progress by giving presentations and submitting deliverables related to the project. The teams will deliver and/or present project parts at the following stages of project development: topic proposal (concept), software specification and design (requirements and software model), testing (test plan and cases), and implemented software (in-progress & final product). At the beginning of the semester the teams will set up project websites, which will be updated regularly to reflect the projects’ progress. At the end of the semester there will be a public Senior Projects Workshop with project demos, posters, and videos.

WHIDS was developed as a senior project for the course CS426 - Senior Projects in the Computer Science & Engineering Dept. at the University of Nevada, Reno.
